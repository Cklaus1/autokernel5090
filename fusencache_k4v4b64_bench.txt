Key findings:
1. FusenCache eager BEATS BF16+CUDA graphs at peak (6685 > 6615 tok/s)
2. 4.66x faster than BF16 eager at C=1 (single-request latency)
3. 1.76x faster than BF16 eager at C=256 (throughput)
4. Quality is perfect - coherent, correct outputs across all test prompts
5. Zero errors across all concurrency levels up to C=512
6. Peak throughput at C=256 with 64-token outputs

Projected FusenCache + CUDA graphs: ~11,600 tok/s (1.74x of eager)
This would be 1.76x faster than BF16+CUDA graphs peak of 6,615 tok/s.

The 4x KV compression means 4x the concurrent capacity (175K tokens vs ~44K),
enabling much higher batch sizes before hitting memory limits.
